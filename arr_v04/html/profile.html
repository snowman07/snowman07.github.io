No matter how I plan my career, if it’s not for me, I won’t get it. To make the story short, I ended up in the software side of the industry. Personally, this was a tough pathway, but I find it challenging and interesting.

When you stop learning, you stop growing Since I earned my degree as a Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering from Polytechnic University of the Philippines, I had been enthusiastically working in the IT industry. To keep myself relevant in the software space and for professional growth, I decided to leave the workforce and go back to school. I enrolled to a Diploma program in Digital Media and IT with concentration in General Software Development from Northern Alberta Institute of Technology (NAIT) where I gained knowledge and skills on the technologies used in frontend and backend software/web development. But learning doesn’t stop on the four corners of the classroom. As a firm believer of continuous learning, I always find time learning about latest technologies that are available online and trying to improve continually so my skills won’t get obsolete.
